10 Frequently Asked National Parks Questions

Is it okay to feed the animals?
No, wild animals are very dangerous. You should keep your distance.
What if they look friendly?
They are not. Even the cute ones
Why would God make bears look so huggable if I weren't supposed to hug one?
You'll have to ask God that one. We are straying off topic. This is an FAQ about the whole park, not just the wildlife.
Can I start a fire?
You can start a fire, as long as you put it out. That is a perfectly acceptable outdoor activity. Unless we are under a fire watch, which happens more and more because we're rolling back regulations on industry... ah, this isn't the place. Next question.
Who owns the National Forest?
Technically, if you pay taxes... You do!
So all of the huggable bears and puppy wolves are mine?
No...
But you said I own them.
Not a question, but I'll answer anyway. We share the land. The animals, however, are wild and can be governed by no man.
Who is going to stop me if I domesticate, say, a bald eagle?
The eagle may try and stop you. With it's very sharp talons.
Why are there so many rules around what we can and can't do with the wildlife.
Every rule in the National Forest is written in blood. The park rangers will not always be there to enforce said rules. Do with that info what you must.
So I CAN feed the animals?
Do what you must, but don't say I didn't warn you.
